# Kalibra Evaluation Engine Implementation Plan

## About This Plan

This document is the implementation plan for the **Evaluation Engine**, the domain
that measures Kalibra against its own documented claims. It consumes the preserved
evidence records held by the
[Evidence Engine](KALIBRA_EVIDENCE_ENGINE_IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN_v1.0.md) and reports
how far the system's claims are supported — along distinct dimensions, with named
failure categories, and never beyond the evidence.

It is an architecture-first plan: it fixes the permanent engineering boundary of
the Evaluation Engine — what it owns, what it refuses, what crosses its seams, and
how it is validated — without choosing how any of it is built.

The plan deliberately does **not** select analytics libraries, databases, or
model-training workflows, and does **not** define final numeric benchmark targets.
Those belong to later phases and to other domains. This document defines the
boundary those later choices must respect. It governs *what must be measured and to
what standard* — drawing the standard from
[`docs/KALIBRA_EVALUATION_METHODOLOGY_v1.0.md`](KALIBRA_EVALUATION_METHODOLOGY_v1.0.md)
— not *how any measurement is computed*.

Throughout, **must** and **must not** express binding engineering obligations,
consistent with the language of
[`docs/KALIBRA_ENGINEERING_PLAN_v1.0.md`](KALIBRA_ENGINEERING_PLAN_v1.0.md) and
[`docs/KALIBRA_SYSTEM_REQUIREMENTS_v1.0.md`](KALIBRA_SYSTEM_REQUIREMENTS_v1.0.md).

---

## 1. Purpose

The Evaluation Engine measures Kalibra against its own claims and surfaces how the
system can fail (Engineering Plan §Evaluation Engine). It is the domain that turns
the Evaluation Methodology's standard — *no claim is made until it has been
demonstrated* — into reported, evidence-backed findings.

Its purpose is to assess the system along **separate dimensions** — detection
quality, calibration, uncertainty quality, review quality, and drift response — so
that strength in one cannot disguise weakness in another, and to **name the failure
categories** rather than collapse them into a single flattering figure (Methodology
§Evaluation Dimensions, §Failure Categories).

The Evaluation Engine draws **only on recorded, reproducible evidence**, so every
measurement it reports can be regenerated by an observer who does not trust the
system. It produces findings; it produces no inspection, no qualification, no
review, and no new evidence of system behaviour. Where evidence is missing or weak,
it reports that plainly and narrows the corresponding claim rather than inventing a
result (Methodology §Evidence Requirements; Requirement R7).

---

## 2. Responsibilities

The Evaluation Engine **must**:

- **Consume preserved evidence records.** Draw its inputs from the records held by
  the Evidence Engine, and measure only from that recorded evidence (Engineering
  Plan §Engineering Dependencies; E-records).
- **Measure only from recorded evidence.** Base every reported finding on preserved
  records; it must not measure anything it cannot trace back to evidence.
- **Keep evaluation dimensions separate.** Assess detection quality, calibration,
  uncertainty quality, review quality, and drift response as distinct dimensions
  that are never merged (Methodology §Evaluation Dimensions).
- **Report detection quality separately from calibration quality.** Being a
  competent inspector and being a calibrated one are reported apart.
- **Report uncertainty quality separately from review quality.** Whether doubt
  tracks error and whether the right cases are deferred are reported apart.
- **Report drift response separately from all other dimensions.** Whether caution
  rises under drift is reported on its own.
- **Surface named failure categories.** Distinguish and report the methodology's
  failure categories — missed defects, false alarms, confident errors, misplaced
  uncertainty, mislocalized defects, unresponsive drift — rather than collapse them
  (Methodology §Failure Categories).
- **Preserve the difference between missing evidence and poor performance.** Report
  absent evidence as absence and weak results as weakness; never convert one into
  the other (Methodology §Evidence Requirements; E5).
- **Prevent flattering aggregates from hiding weakness.** Never let a single summary
  figure conceal a weak dimension or a failure category (Methodology §Failure
  Categories; AGENTS.md Trust & Reliability Rules).
- **Emit evaluation reports as evidence-backed artifacts.** Produce reports that are
  reproducible, inspectable, and traceable back to the preserved records they rest
  on (Methodology §Evidence Requirements; E2, E3, P2).

These responsibilities are the complete remit of the engine. Anything not listed
here is, by design, not its job.

## 3. Explicit Non-Responsibilities

The Evaluation Engine **must not** take on any of the following. Each belongs to a
named domain or a deferred architecture, and the boundary is load-bearing:

- **Inspect images directly.** It must not examine visual inputs. *(Inspection
  Engine.)*
- **Create raw inspection judgements.** It must not produce judgements,
  localizations, or anomaly measures. *(Inspection Engine.)*
- **Calibrate confidence.** It must not produce or alter calibrated confidence; it
  measures whether existing confidence was calibrated. *(Trust Qualification
  Engine.)*
- **Qualify trust.** It must not sort decisions into accept/review/reject/abstain.
  *(Trust Qualification Engine.)*
- **Route or perform human review.** It must not defer cases or adjudicate them; it
  measures whether deferral was appropriate. *(Human Review Engine.)*
- **Modify evidence records.** It must not mutate, overwrite, or correct any record;
  it reads them. *(Evidence Engine owns the records.)*
- **Fabricate missing evidence.** It must not invent, infer, or synthesise evidence
  that the upstream engines did not produce.
- **Collapse evaluation into a single flattering score.** It must not reduce its
  findings to one aggregate that hides a weak dimension or failure category.
- **Train or update models.** It must not train, retrain, recalibrate, or update any
  model.
- **Use reviewer decisions as feedback loops.** It must not route reviewer decisions
  (or any finding) back into model updates (Exclusion X5).
- **Treat prototype visuals or synthetic overlays as performance evidence.**
  Illustrative assets must never be measured or reported as demonstrated model
  performance (Foundation §Engineering Philosophy; AGENTS.md Trust & Reliability
  Rules).

The engine must also remain within Kalibra's constraints: offline, batch, and
reproducible, with no live, streaming, hosted, or continuously operating behaviour
and no operational alerting or scheduling (Constraints C1, C2; Exclusions X1–X4).
It measures the **present** system as it is, not a future, broader one (Methodology
§Methodology Boundaries).

## 4. Inputs

The Evaluation Engine accepts a single kind of input:

- **Preserved evidence records (read-only).** The durable records held by the
  Evidence Engine — inspection evidence records, trust qualification evidence
  records, review evidence records — together with their immutable chain links and
  explicit absence markers. (Evidence Engine plan §5 and §8, Contracts B–E.)

Properties the input **must** satisfy:

- Records are consumed **read-only**; the engine reads the preserved store and the
  chain (source input → raw inspection result → trust qualification → review case →
  reviewer decision), never writing to it (core boundary).
- The provenance of each record is intact, so every finding can be traced back to
  the exact records it rests on (Methodology §Evidence Requirements: self-contained;
  E3).
- **Absence markers are first-class inputs.** A recorded absence is read as
  absence — it is an input the engine must report as missing evidence, not silently
  treat as a value (Methodology §Evidence Requirements: honest about absence; E5).
- Records are drawn from a fixed body (offline/batch); there is no live or streaming
  source (C1, C4, X8).

What the engine **must not** require or assume:

- Any access to the raw image or to intake beyond the source-input *reference* in
  the records.
- That every record exists. The evidence may be partial; the engine reports a
  partial, honestly-stated result rather than filling gaps (Methodology §Success
  Criteria).
- Any prototype visual or synthetic overlay as a stand-in for recorded model
  outputs (core boundary).

This plan fixes the engine's *contract* with the evidence, not its representation.
A stable reference set against which the system is measured must be fixed for a
given evaluation (P5); the engine consumes such a reference, it does not invent one.

## 5. Outputs

The Evaluation Engine produces:

- **Dimensioned findings.** A separate finding for each dimension — detection
  quality, calibration, uncertainty quality, review quality, drift response — kept
  distinct and never merged (Methodology §Evaluation Dimensions).
- **Named failure-category findings.** Explicit reporting of missed defects, false
  alarms, confident errors, misplaced uncertainty, mislocalized defects, and
  unresponsive drift, each named rather than absorbed into an aggregate (Methodology
  §Failure Categories).
- **Absence and limitation disclosures.** Explicit statements wherever the evidence
  for a dimension or category is missing or weak, with the corresponding claim
  narrowed or withdrawn to match (Methodology §Evidence Requirements; E5, R7).
- **Evidence-backed evaluation reports.** Reports that bind each finding to the
  preserved records it rests on, so an assertion and its justification are never
  separated, and that are regenerable from the fixed starting point (E2, E3, P2).

Properties the plan fixes:

- **Findings are traceable.** Every reported finding traces back to preserved
  evidence records; nothing is reported that cannot be so traced (core boundary).
- **Dimensions stay separate.** No output collapses the five dimensions, and no
  single figure is permitted to hide a weak dimension or a failure category (core
  boundary; Methodology §Failure Categories).
- **Missing ≠ weak.** Missing evidence is reported as missing evidence; weak
  performance is reported as weak performance; neither is converted into the other
  (core boundary).
- **Reports are read-only products.** Producing a report mutates no evidence record
  and no upstream artifact (core boundary).
- **No claim exceeds its evidence.** Where support is partial, the claim is partial
  and honestly stated; where absent, the claim is withdrawn (R7; Methodology
  §Success Criteria).
- Reports are themselves **emitted as evidence-backed artifacts** and are
  regenerable from a fixed starting point (P1, P2, C2).

The Evaluation Engine's reports are the final inspectable expression of whether
Kalibra's reliability claims hold — measured, separated, named, and never inflated.

## 6. Domain Boundaries

The Evaluation Engine sits last in Kalibra's directed flow and reads from one
domain.

**Inbound seam — Evidence → Evaluation.**
The engine consumes the records held by the Evidence Engine and reports its findings
as further inspectable evidence (Engineering Plan §Evaluation Engine). What crosses
this seam is read-only preserved evidence — records, links, and absence markers. The
engine reads; it never writes back, and it draws no measurement that is not grounded
in those records.

**Outbound — Evaluation reports as evidence-backed artifacts.**
The engine's reports are produced as inspectable, reproducible artifacts. Later
prototype and product surfaces may present these reports, but the engine does not own
those surfaces' UI.

Boundaries the engine must hold:

- **Evaluation consumes evidence; it does not generate evidence** of system
  behaviour. Its reports are findings *about* recorded evidence, traceable back to
  it — not new records of what the system did (core boundary).
- It must keep the five dimensions distinct and must not collapse them, in keeping
  with the rule that evaluation must keep distinct dimensions separate and not
  flatter (AGENTS.md Trust & Reliability Rules).
- It must not inspect, calibrate, qualify, route, review, mutate evidence, train, or
  feed back; responsibilities must not bleed across the seam for convenience
  (Engineering Plan §Engineering Principles).
- It must measure the **present** system, not a future, broader one (Engineering
  Plan §Evaluation Engine; Methodology §Methodology Boundaries).
- It must remain offline/batch/reproducible and introduce no live operation
  (C1, C2, X1).

## 7. Internal Processing Stages

The engine's internal work is described here as **responsibility stages**, not as an
algorithm, and not as a set of formulas. The stages fix *what must happen in order*,
not *how* any measurement is computed. No analytics library, statistic, or numeric
target is chosen here.

1. **Evidence intake.** Read the relevant preserved evidence records, their chain
   links, and their absence markers from the Evidence Engine, against a fixed
   reference set. Treat everything read as read-only.

2. **Evidence sufficiency check.** For each dimension and failure category, determine
   whether the recorded evidence is present and sufficient to support a finding.
   Where it is absent or weak, mark the dimension/category for an explicit absence or
   limitation disclosure rather than a fabricated value (E5).

3. **Per-dimension assessment.** Assess each dimension separately, drawing only on
   the records relevant to it:
   - **Detection quality** — whether defective and sound inputs are distinguished and
     defects located, from recorded judgements and localizations.
   - **Calibration** — whether recorded confidence corresponds to recorded outcomes.
   - **Uncertainty quality** — whether recorded uncertainty/abstention concentrates
     recorded error.
   - **Review quality** — whether the recorded deferred set is weighted toward the
     difficult, ambiguous, error-prone cases.
   - **Drift response** — whether recorded caution rises with recorded drift under
     controlled variation.
   Each assessment is kept in its own dimension and is not merged with another.

4. **Failure-category surfacing.** Identify and name the methodology's failure
   categories from the records — missed defects, false alarms, confident errors,
   misplaced uncertainty, mislocalized defects, unresponsive drift — keeping each
   distinct.

5. **Comparative assessment where relevant.** Where the evidence records a before/
   after state for a step intended to improve a property, assess the change from
   those paired records, so improvement is demonstrated and not asserted (E4;
   Methodology §Calibration).

6. **Honest reconciliation.** Reconcile findings with the evidence: narrow or
   withdraw any claim the evidence does not support, keep missing-evidence and
   weak-performance reports distinct, and ensure no aggregate hides a weak dimension
   or category (R7; Methodology §Success Criteria).

7. **Report assembly.** Assemble the dimensioned findings, named failure categories,
   absence/limitation disclosures, and their bindings back to the preserved records
   into one evidence-backed evaluation report.

Ordering and boundary obligations:

- The sufficiency check (stage 2) must precede assessment; the engine never assesses
  a dimension whose evidence it has not confirmed present, and never substitutes a
  value for absence.
- No stage may inspect images, create or alter upstream results, qualify, route,
  review, train, or feed back. If a stage appears to need any of these, the need
  belongs to a different domain and the seam, not this engine.
- No stage may treat prototype visuals or synthetic overlays as performance evidence
  (core boundary).
- All stages operate offline and in batch over a fixed body of records; no stage
  introduces live, streaming, or continuously operating behaviour (C1, X1).
- Every stage must be deterministic with respect to the fixed evidence and fixed
  reference set, so reports are regenerable (C2, P2, P5).

## 8. Data Contracts

This section fixes the **shape and obligations** of what the engine consumes and
produces, expressed as abstract contracts. Concrete types, encodings, field names,
statistics, and report formats are deliberately left to implementation; only the
obligations below are binding.

**Contract A — Preserved Evidence (consumed, read-only).**
- The Evidence Engine's preserved records, chain links, and absence markers (Evidence
  Engine plan Contracts B–E).
- Consumed against a fixed reference set for the evaluation (P5).
- Treated as **immutable and read-only**: the engine reads and must not mutate,
  overwrite, or correct anything.

**Contract B — Dimension Finding (produced).**
- A finding for exactly one of {detection quality, calibration, uncertainty quality,
  review quality, drift response}.
- Carries its binding to the preserved records it rests on (traceability).
- Carries an explicit status: supported, partial (narrowed), or unsupported/absent —
  never an invented value standing in for missing evidence.
- Must not be merged with a finding for another dimension.

**Contract C — Failure-Category Finding (produced).**
- A named finding for one of {missed defects, false alarms, confident errors,
  misplaced uncertainty, mislocalized defects, unresponsive drift}.
- Carries its binding to the preserved records it rests on.
- Is reported distinctly; it must not be absorbed into an aggregate that hides it.

**Contract D — Absence / Limitation Disclosure (produced).**
- An explicit statement that the evidence for a dimension or category is missing or
  weak, with the corresponding claim narrowed or withdrawn (E5, R7).
- Distinct from a weak-performance finding: missing evidence and weak performance are
  never represented as the same thing.

**Contract E — Evaluation Report (emitted).**
- Binds dimension findings, failure-category findings, and absence/limitation
  disclosures together, each traceable to preserved records (E2, E3).
- Is regenerable from the fixed starting point and reference set (P2, P5).
- Faithfully represents support: it must not present a finding as stronger than its
  evidence allows, and must not present prototype visuals or synthetic overlays as
  performance (R7; core boundary).

Contract invariants:

- **Read-only consumption.** Evaluation mutates no evidence record and no upstream
  artifact (core boundary).
- **Dimensional separation.** The five dimensions are reported distinctly; no
  contract merges them (Methodology §Evaluation Dimensions).
- **Missing ≠ weak.** Absence disclosures and weak-performance findings are distinct
  contract objects (core boundary).
- **No flattering aggregate.** No produced object reduces findings to a single figure
  that conceals a weak dimension or category (Methodology §Failure Categories).
- **Traceability.** Every produced finding binds back to the preserved records it
  rests on (E3).
- **No fabrication, no feedback.** Nothing is invented to fill absence, and no finding
  is routed into a model update (E5; Exclusion X5).

## 9. Failure Modes

The engine must distinguish **reported findings about the system** (including
reported weakness and reported absence — all normal) from **engine failures** (the
engine cannot read, assess, or report from the evidence). A failure is never silently
turned into a finding, and the difference between *missing evidence* and *poor
performance* is never blurred.

Identified failure modes and required handling:

- **Missing or insufficient evidence for a dimension/category.** This is **not** an
  engine failure and **not** a poor score: the engine reports an explicit absence/
  limitation disclosure and narrows or withdraws the claim (E5, R7). It is listed
  here to fix that absence must be reported as absence, never converted into a score
  (core boundary).
- **Pressure to fabricate.** Any path that would invent, infer, or synthesise
  evidence to fill a gap is a boundary violation to be prevented (core boundary).
- **Dimension collapse.** Any path that would merge the five dimensions, or reduce
  findings to a single flattering aggregate that hides a weak dimension or category,
  is a boundary violation to be prevented (core boundary; Methodology §Failure
  Categories).
- **Missing-vs-weak conflation.** Any path that would represent missing evidence as a
  weak score, or a weak score as missing evidence, is a defect to be prevented (core
  boundary).
- **Attempted mutation of evidence.** Any path that would alter a preserved record or
  an upstream artifact is a defect to be prevented; consumption is read-only (core
  boundary).
- **Prototype-as-performance.** Any path that would measure or report prototype
  visuals or synthetic overlays as model performance is a boundary violation to be
  prevented (core boundary).
- **Attempted feedback or training.** Any path that would route a finding or reviewer
  decision into retraining, recalibration, or model update is a boundary violation to
  be prevented (Exclusion X5).
- **Untraceable finding.** A finding that cannot be bound back to preserved records
  must not be emitted; the engine must surface this rather than report an unsupported
  result (E3).
- **Non-reproducibility.** Re-running over the same fixed evidence and reference set
  yields a different report, indicating hidden state. This violates C2/P2/P5 and must
  be surfaced as a defect, not tolerated.
- **Introduction of live operation.** Any path that would make evaluation live,
  streaming, or continuously operating is out of bounds and must be prevented
  (C1, X1).

General obligations for all failure modes:

- Failures must be **explicit and inspectable**, never swallowed.
- A failure, an absence, and a weak result must never be **disguised as one
  another**; honesty about absence and weakness is the engine's core duty
  (Methodology §Evaluation Philosophy).
- Failure handling must not import other domains' responsibilities (it must not, for
  example, re-inspect or recalibrate to fill a gap — it only surfaces the failure or
  absence).

This plan fixes *which* failures must be handled and *how they must be treated*; the
concrete error mechanisms are implementation choices.

## 10. Validation Strategy

Validation here means confirming the engine honours its boundary and contracts —
**not** fixing the numeric benchmark targets the engine will report, which are out of
scope for this plan. No final benchmark targets are defined.

The engine must be validated against:

- **Evidence-only measurement.** Every finding traces back to preserved evidence
  records; nothing is reported that cannot be so traced (core boundary; E3).
- **Read-only consumption.** Evidence records and upstream artifacts are unchanged
  after evaluation (core boundary).
- **Dimensional separation.** Detection, calibration, uncertainty, review, and drift
  are reported as distinct dimensions and never merged (Methodology §Evaluation
  Dimensions).
- **Named failure categories.** Missed defects, false alarms, confident errors,
  misplaced uncertainty, mislocalized defects, and unresponsive drift are reported
  distinctly, not absorbed into an aggregate (Methodology §Failure Categories).
- **Missing ≠ weak.** Missing evidence is reported as absence and weak performance as
  weakness; neither is converted into the other (core boundary; E5).
- **No flattering aggregate.** No output reduces findings to a single figure that
  conceals a weak dimension or category (core boundary).
- **No fabrication.** No path invents evidence to fill a gap (core boundary).
- **No prototype-as-performance.** Prototype visuals and synthetic overlays are never
  reported as model performance (core boundary).
- **No training or feedback.** No finding or reviewer decision is routed into a model
  update (Exclusion X5).
- **Present-system scope.** The engine measures the present system, not a future one
  (Methodology §Methodology Boundaries).
- **Offline / batch / reproducible.** The engine introduces no live operation, and the
  same fixed evidence and reference set yield the same report on re-run (C1, C2, P2,
  P5, X1).
- **Failure handling.** Each failure mode in §9 is surfaced explicitly and never
  disguised; absence, weakness, and engine failure remain distinct.

Validation must rest on **the reports and their bindings to preserved evidence**, so
an observer can verify conformance without trusting the engine's word, consistent
with Kalibra's evidence-before-assertion philosophy.

## 11. Testing Strategy

Testing fixes *what must be demonstrated* about the engine; it does not fix an
analytics library, database, harness, or tooling, and it does not assert particular
numeric benchmark outcomes.

The engine's test suite must demonstrate, at minimum:

- **Traceability tests.** Every emitted finding binds back to the preserved records
  it rests on; a finding that cannot be traced is not emitted.
- **Read-only tests.** After evaluation, evidence records and upstream artifacts are
  byte-for-byte unchanged.
- **Separation tests.** The five dimensions are reported as distinct findings and
  cannot be merged into one; this is asserted structurally.
- **Failure-category tests.** Each named category can be reported distinctly and is
  not absorbed into an aggregate.
- **Missing-vs-weak tests.** Given evidence with a recorded absence for a dimension,
  the engine reports an explicit absence disclosure — not a low score; given present
  but weak-performance evidence, it reports weakness — not absence. The two cases are
  shown to produce distinct outputs.
- **No-flattering-aggregate tests.** No output reduces findings to a single figure
  that hides a weak dimension or category; asserted as *must-not-exist*.
- **No-fabrication tests.** No path invents evidence to fill a gap; asserted as
  *must-not-exist*.
- **No-prototype-as-performance tests.** Prototype visuals / synthetic overlays cannot
  be reported as model performance; asserted as *must-not-exist*.
- **No-feedback / no-training tests.** No path routes a finding or reviewer decision
  into a model update (Exclusion X5); asserted as *must-not-exist*.
- **Boundary tests (negative).** The engine exposes no inspection, calibration,
  qualification, routing, review, or evidence-mutation behaviour; asserted as
  *must-not-exist*, guarding the seam.
- **Comparative tests.** Where before/after evidence is present, the engine assesses
  the change from the paired records rather than asserting improvement (E4).
- **Reproducibility tests.** Re-running over identical fixed evidence and reference
  set yields an identical report.
- **Failure-mode tests.** Each failure mode in §9 (missing/insufficient evidence,
  fabrication pressure, dimension collapse, missing-vs-weak conflation, mutation
  attempt, prototype-as-performance, attempted feedback/training, untraceable
  finding, non-reproducibility, attempted live operation) is provoked and shown to be
  surfaced or reported correctly and never disguised.

Testing principles:

- Tests assert the engine's **boundary and contracts**, not particular benchmark
  numbers. No test in this domain may bake in a final numeric target as a pass
  condition for the *system*; tests check that findings are reported honestly and
  separately, not that the system hits a score.
- Tests must use a **fixed body of evidence and a fixed reference set** so they are
  themselves reproducible (C2, P5).
- Tests should be small and focused, each covering one obligation, consistent with
  Kalibra's coding rules (small, testable modules).

The concrete test code, fixtures, and runner are produced during implementation, not
in this plan.

## 12. Future Extension Points

These are points where later phases attach to the Evaluation Engine. Naming them
fixes the boundary now; none grants licence to absorb another domain's responsibility
or open a deferred capability.

- **Measurement methods are replaceable.** Because this plan fixes the engine's
  contracts and seams but not its statistics or measurement techniques, the methods
  chosen in a later phase can be implemented and later replaced without changing the
  engine's boundary, provided dimensions stay separate, findings stay traceable, and
  absence stays distinct from weakness (Contracts A–E).
- **Numeric targets are a later, owner-set decision.** Final numeric benchmark targets
  are deliberately undefined here. Setting them is a later phase governed by the
  Methodology's standard of proof; the engine's job is honest, separated, traceable
  reporting regardless of the targets chosen.
- **New dimensions or categories require a documentation change.** The five dimensions
  and the named failure categories come from the Methodology. Adding to them is an
  owner-set change to the public documentation, not something the engine introduces on
  its own (Methodology §Methodology Boundaries; AGENTS.md Documentation Rules).
- **Report presentation builds on emitted reports.** Later prototype and product
  surfaces may present the engine's evidence-backed reports; that UI is the extension
  and is not owned here.
- **Comparative evaluation can be extended.** Additional before/after comparisons can
  be assessed from paired evidence records (E4) without changing the engine's
  responsibility, provided each finding stays traceable and honestly stated.

Deferred scope remains deferred (Engineering Plan §Engineering Boundaries; AGENTS.md
Scope Protection). An extension that would let the Evaluation Engine inspect, qualify,
route, review, mutate evidence, fabricate results, collapse dimensions into a
flattering score, train or update models, feed reviewer decisions back, treat
prototype visuals as performance, or operate live is, by definition, out of bounds and
belongs to a later architecture, not to this engine.

## Closing Statement

This plan fixes the permanent engineering boundary of the Kalibra Evaluation Engine.
The engine measures the system only from preserved evidence; keeps detection,
calibration, uncertainty, review, and drift as separate dimensions; names the failure
categories rather than burying them; holds missing evidence apart from weak
performance; refuses to flatter the system with a single aggregate; and emits reports
that trace back, claim by claim, to the records they rest on. It inspects nothing,
qualifies nothing, reviews nothing, mutates no evidence, trains no model, and never
dresses an illustration as a result.

Engineered to this boundary, the Evaluation Engine is the final guarantor of
Kalibra's discipline: every reliability claim the system makes is measured against its
own evidence, separated so strength cannot hide weakness, and narrowed the moment the
evidence runs out — so that whenever Kalibra says it knows when not to trust itself,
an observer can check the claim and see exactly where it holds and where it does not.
